### [2:30 - 5:00] THE INVESTIGATION - PHASE 1: THE SPECTRUM SQUEEZE
**[VISUAL: Host showing a heatmap of 5G signal strength in a Tier-1 Indian city. We see 'Dead Zones' in every major residential block.]**

**HOST:** "Step 1: The Squeeze. India’s 5G rollout used the **3.5 GHz band**—the 'Golden Mean' of speed and range. But the government kept the base prices so high that telcos couldn't afford enough 'Width.' It’s like a 10-lane highway where 8 lanes are closed because the toll was too expensive. We are all crammed into the remaining 2 lanes."

**[VISUAL: Comparison of 'Spectrum per Million Users' in India vs. USA.]**

**HOST:** "The 'Hidden Agenda' of the 2026 spectrum policy was revenue maximization, not user experience. The telcos are forced to 'Over-Subscribe' each tower just to pay off the debt from the auction. You are paying for a 'Premium' service, but you are sharing the bandwidth with 10,000 other people. In 2026, 5G isn't a 'Speed Boost'; it’s just 4G with a better marketing department."

### [5:00 - 7:30] THE CORE CHALLENGE: THE ELEVATOR TEST
**[VISUAL: Host is in a modern high-rise in Mumbai. He enters an elevator. The 5G signal vanishes instantly.]**

**HOST:** "The 'Wall' of 5G. 5G signals operate at higher frequencies, which means they can't penetrate concrete, glass, or even a heavy rainstorm. To make 5G work in a city like Mumbai, you need **Small Cells** every 100 meters. But look at the street."

**[VISUAL: Host walking down a street, looking for 'Small Cells' (hidden on poles/buildings). He only finds 4G towers.]**

**HOST:** "The rollout is 'Surface-Level.' The telcos are bragging about 'City-Wide' coverage, but they are only covering the 'Air.' Indoors, where 80% of data is consumed, you are still falling back to 4G or even 2G. The 'Hidden Agenda' of the telcos is to prioritize 'Outdoor Benchmarks' to win government awards, while ignoring the 'Indoor Reality' of their customers."
